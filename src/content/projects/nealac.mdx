## What it is

NEALAC Learning Hub is a continuing-education platform built for serious course consumption. The goal of the work was not to turn it into something flashy. It was to make the experience cleaner, easier to follow, and more comfortable to use over long sessions.

That meant focusing on the player itself: how video, transcript, chapters, quizzes, and flashcards work together once a learner is already engaged.

## UX improvements that matter in practice

Many of the strongest wins on NEALAC are the kind users feel immediately even when they cannot name them.

- transcript auto-scroll behavior was fixed and cleaned up
- sticky navigation reduced layout friction during longer lessons
- duplicate header clutter was removed
- glossary formatting was normalized for easier reading
- flashcards gained better shuffle and keyboard behavior
- multi-select quiz submission behavior was corrected
- caption styling and custom controls got product-level polish

These are not decorative changes. They directly improve comprehension, pacing, and confidence while someone is actually trying to learn.

## Design decisions

### Make the player do less fighting

The course player should support the lesson, not compete with it. Layout decisions, sticky navigation, and cleaner interaction patterns reduce the amount of UI management a learner has to do while studying.

### Treat transcripts like a first-class surface

For many courses, the transcript is not secondary. It is how people search, review, and stay oriented. Designing around that reality changes the quality of the whole product.

### Keep accessibility in the room

Keyboard support, clearer states, and tighter caption behavior are the kinds of details that make a platform feel cared for. They also make it easier to trust.

## Build notes

NEALAC is a good example of how product polish often comes from many focused improvements rather than one dramatic redesign. Better study flow, fewer rough edges, and a stronger player experience add up to a platform that feels more usable every time someone returns to it.
